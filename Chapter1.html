Lewis Structure

Lewis Structures show the bonding between atoms and unpaired electrons.

  • Bonds are lines; each line is a bond.
  • Dots are unpaired electrons.
  • Structures are drawn to satisfy octet rule (if possible).

Atoms have a tendency to form a certain number of bonds, this is refered to Valency.

Formal Charge

Formal Charge is the charge on an atom if bonds are prefectly covalent. Formula to find formal charge is:

Formal Charge = # of valence electrons - (# of sticks + # of dots).

  • Sum of the Formal Charges = overall charge
  • Formal Charges are often on atoms that Don't observe standard valency.

Structure Representations

Molecular structures are often represented in 3 ways:

  1. Kekule: All hydrogens (H) and bonds are shown.

  2. Condensed: In condensed notation, Hydrogens (H) are grouped together.

  3. Bond-line: In bond-line notation, Carbons and Hydrogens are not directly shown.

    • Carbons are at vertices and ends.
    • Hydrogens are implicit.

VSEPR Theory

VSEPR theory predict the 3D shapes of molecules. With VSEPR theory:

  • Electron domains repel.
  • Wedge-dash notation is used to show groups that point out of the page (wedges) and into the page (dashes).

Valence Bond Theory and hybridization

Electrons are in atomic orbitals

Valence Bond Theory states that bonds form when atomic orbitals with unpaired electrons overlap.

Atomic orbitals can be mixed to genrate hybrid orbitals. Hybridization is related to electron geometry

The overlap of S orbitals and/or hybrid orbitals yeilds σ bonds. The overlap of P orbitals yeilds π bonds.
