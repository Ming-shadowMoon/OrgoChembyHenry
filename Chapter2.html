Resonance Structure

Structural Features for Resonance

There are 3 main structural features to look for in order to determine if resonance can be drawn:

  1. Alternating double&single bonds. (ex. all around a ring)

  2. Lone pair and/or charge next to a π bond.

  3. Lone pair next to positive charge.

Arrow Pushing Mechanism

For drawing resonance, electrons can be moved via arrow pushing to generate resonance structure.

  1. Electrons get "pushed" to where they aren't; usually get pushed between 2 atoms near a π bond or positive charge.
  2. π electrons get pushed out if octet is exceeded.
  3. Fill in formal charges in new resonance structure.

  1. Maximize the octet rule
