Bronsted-Lowry

In Bronsted-Lowry acid-base definition, acids donate proton and bases accept proton

Ka and pKa

The strength of an acid is quantified by its Ka/pKa value.

  • Stronger acid have large Ka / lower pKa

pKa can determined the equilibrium constant (Keq) of an acid-base reaction.

Stability of Structure

In chemistry, energy and stability are inversely related. Electronic factors affect the stability of a molecule: the build-up of charge distabilizes and the spread of charge stabilizes.

Periodic Trends that affect the stability of molecules:

  1. Electronegativity:
    • Negative charge (-) is more stable on more Electronegative atom
    • Positive charge (+) is more stable on less Electronegative atom
    • This trend is dominant for the same row elements.
  2. Atomic Radius: Charge is more stable on larger atom.
    • Larger atoms can stabilize negative charges due to distributed electron density.
    • This trend is dominant for same group element
  3. Inductive Effect: electronegative atoms are able to stabilizes nearby negative charges (-)
    • The effect drops quickly with distance
    • The effect is additive
  4. Resonance: charge is stabilize since the resonance strucutre delocalize the electron density (charge is more spread out)
  5. Hybridization: more s orbital increases electronegativity
    • The more the s orbital, the closer electrons can get to the nucleus

Favorable Reactions and Mechanism

A favorable reaction result in weaker (more stable) acids(&bases).

This mean a reaction is favorable if the conjugate acid is weaker than acid or conjugate base is weaker than base.

In a favorable reaction, the equilibrium constant (Keq) would be greater and vice versa in unfavorable reaction.

In drawing mechanism of an acid-base reaction, we can use arrow pushing to show the transfer of election as bonds are made/broken.
